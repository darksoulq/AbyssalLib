# Default Animations
<link-summary>Reference guide for built-in particle transformers and animations</link-summary>

Animations (or `Transformers`) manipulate the coordinates generated by a shape over time. Below are the built-in animations accessible via the `Animations` and `Billboarding` utility classes.

### Spin
**Methods:** `Animations.spinX()`, `Animations.spinY()`, `Animations.spinZ()`

Rotates the entire particle shape around a specific axis over a set duration.

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>totalDegrees</code></td>
<td>The total amount of rotation to perform (e.g., <code>360</code> for a full spin).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>duration</code></td>
<td>The lifespan of the animation sequence in server ticks.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>easing</code></td>
<td>The <code>Easing</code> mathematical function dictating the rotation curve.</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Scale
**Method:** `Animations.scale()`

Grows or shrinks the particle shape from a starting size to an ending size.

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>startScale</code></td>
<td>The initial scale multiplier applied at tick 0.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>endScale</code></td>
<td>The final scale multiplier applied at the end of the duration.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>duration</code></td>
<td>The total time in ticks for the scaling process to complete.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>easing</code></td>
<td>The <code>Easing</code> mathematical function dictating the growth curve.</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Translate
**Method:** `Animations.translate()`

Moves the particle shape towards a target directional offset.

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>totalOffset</code></td>
<td>The final <code>Vector</code> directional offset applied by the end of the duration.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>duration</code></td>
<td>The total time in ticks for the translation to complete.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>easing</code></td>
<td>The <code>Easing</code> mathematical function dictating the movement speed.</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Breathe
**Method:** `Animations.breathe()`

Creates a cyclical "breathing" animation that repeatedly pulses the shape's scale using a sine wave.

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>minScale</code></td>
<td>The minimum scale factor at the bottom of the pulse (exhale).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>maxScale</code></td>
<td>The maximum scale factor at the peak of the pulse (inhale).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>duration</code></td>
<td>The number of ticks required to complete one full oscillation loop.</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Billboard Face (Static)
**Method:** `Billboarding.face()`

Calculates a static rotation to make the particle shape face a specific target coordinate once upon initialization.

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>origin</code></td>
<td>The starting <code>Location</code> of the particle effect.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>target</code></td>
<td>The <code>Location</code> the shape should be oriented towards.</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Billboard Face (Dynamic)
**Method:** `Billboarding.faceDynamic()`

Continuously recalculates rotation every tick to ensure the shape is always facing a moving target (e.g., a player's camera).

<table>
<tr>
<th>Parameter</th>
<th>Information</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>originSupplier</code></td>
<td>A <code>Supplier&lt;Location&gt;</code> providing the effect's current origin location.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><code>targetSupplier</code></td>
<td>A <code>Supplier&lt;Location&gt;</code> providing the current target location.</td>
</tr>
</table>
